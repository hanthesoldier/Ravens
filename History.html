History

The Baltimore Ravens are one of the most successful modern franchises in the National Football League, despite being established in 1996 after owner Art Modell controversially relocated the Cleveland Browns to Baltimore, leaving the Browns’ name and history behind in Cleveland; the new team was named in honor of Edgar Allan Poe and his famous poem “The Raven.” From the beginning, the Ravens built their identity around dominant defense, which peaked in 2000 when, led by Ray Lewis, they won Super Bowl XXXV with one of the greatest defenses in NFL history; they remained competitive throughout the 2000s and early 2010s thanks to stars like Ed Reed and Terrell Suggs and the leadership of coach John Harbaugh, eventually winning their second title in Super Bowl XLVII behind quarterback Joe Flacco in a historic matchup against the San Francisco 49ers coached by Jim Harbaugh; in recent years, the team has entered a new era led by Lamar Jackson, whose 2019 MVP season showcased a high-powered offense, and today the Ravens, who play at M&T Bank Stadium, continue to be a consistent playoff contender with a legacy built on defense, strong leadership, and adaptability.

Old Logo

The Old Baltimore Ravens Logo Description

  • The Old Baltimore Ravens logo was designed in 1996 by Fredrick Bouchat when the team was established. It features a stylized raven's head in profile, with a fierce expression and a prominent beak. The raven is depicted in black and purple, with white accents for the eye and beak. The logo is simple yet bold, reflecting the team's identity as a tough and competitive franchise. The use of the raven as the central element of the logo pays homage to Edgar Allan Poe, who is closely associated with Baltimore and is famous for his poem "The Raven."
